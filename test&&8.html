THE RETURNING CITIZEN

Michelle Jones is the Founder and CEO of Spiral Group, Inc. She began the process of developing the NFP organization in April 2015, almost one year after her release from the Logan Correctional facility. Soon after her release, she had attended three well-known job readiness training programs funded by the government.

Michelle began utilizing her skills and experience to make a necessary contribution to society. And, because she had been enrolled in three well-known job readiness training programs, she knew that she would not make the same mistakes she encountered by administrators and instructors when building Spiral Group.

Her goal was to develop training modules pertinent to employment preparation and shorten the length of job readiness training, considerably. After many months of painstaking research; the development and subsequent revision of training modules; and the creation of a comprehensive website, in April 2016, Spiral Group, Inc. and SpiralGroup.org were born.

Currently, Spiral Group, Inc., provides free, online Employment Preparation and Performance Management Training for persosn with criminal histories who are willing to make a commitment to positive change. The NFP's goal is to assist returning citizens in achieving self-sufficiency, while reducing an inclination toward recidivism. The company is dedicated to helping this population of job seekers overcome many obstacles to employment by improving their organizational, time-management, decision-making, interviewing, and soft skills.

MORE ABOUT MICHELLE
Michelle Jones is a Certified ServSafe® Instructor and Registered Examination Proctor with over 20 years' experience in the hospitality and foodservice industries as an hourly worker, cashier supervisor, assistant manager, general manager, trainer, training manager, and franchise business operations consultant.

She has worked with the following organizations: Subway, Portillo's, Quiznos, Dock's Great Fish, Burger King, Chili's, Target, and Jeepers. She opened her first business, RMRC (Restaurant Materials Resource Center), in 1999 where she designed business documents, labels, and posters for the foodservice industry.
