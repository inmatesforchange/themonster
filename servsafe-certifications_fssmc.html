 ILLINOIS FSSMC CERTIFICATION COURSE & EXAM

OVERVIEW
Receive your Food Service Sanitation Manager Certification (FSSMC) with Spiral Group, Inc., and trust ServSafe—the only food safety program with roots in the foodservice industry to help you protect customer health, improve employee performance, and preserve your business reputation.

Preparing food for others is a big responsibility. The steps you follow, or neglect to follow, can adversely affect other people’s health and wellness. Consumers trust that the food they are served in your food establishment is safe to eat. In this class, you will learn how to properly prepare and serve food so that you can maintain that trust.

In the ServSafe Food Protection Manager class, conducted by a Certified ServSafe Instructor, managers learn to implement essential food safety practices and create a culture of food safety. All content and materials are based on actual job tasks identified by foodservice industry experts.

The information imparted to food service managers in this program is based on the FDA food code, food safety research, and years of food sanitation training experience. The program is designed and facilitated to provide sufficient food safety knowledge to protect the public from foodborne illness.

BENEFITS OF THE SERVSAFE® FOOD PROTECTION MANAGER CERTIFICATION:
Validates your professional credibility.
National accreditation as a Food Protection Manager offers you acceptance in all fifty states for regulatory requirements up to 16 hours.
Authenticates that you understand the tasks that employees, managers, and food safety professionals need to know to keep food safe in their establishment.
Your food safety knowledge can be shared with your entire operation.

  
 

An FSSMC is required by Section 750.540 of the Illinois Food Service Sanitation Code, based on a food service establishment’s risk category assigned by the local health department.

The National Restaurant Association (NRA) ServSafe® Food Protection Manager Certification program provides high quality food safety examination materials for the restaurant and food service industry. The ServSafe program is recognized and accepted by more federal, state, and local jurisdictions than any other food safety program. The NRA has awarded more than three million ServSafe Food Protection Manager Certifications.

HOW DOES THE SERVSAFE FOOD HANDLER CERTIFICATE DIFFER FROM THE SERVSAFE® FOOD PROTECTION MANAGER CERTIFICATION?
The ServSafe® Food Handler Certificate verifies basic food safety knowledge and is for individuals in food handler employee-level positions. Upon successful completion of the ANSI ASTM 2659 accredited Food Handler course and 40-question assessment, the employee will receive a Certificate of Achievement from the National Restaurant Association that can be printed from a secure section of the ServSafe® website.

The ServSafe® Food Protection Manager Certification verifies that a manager, or person-in-charge, has sufficient food safety knowledge to protect the public from foodborne illness. Individuals that successfully pass the exam will receive a ServSafe® Food Protection Manager Certification. The ServSafe® Food Protection Manager Certification is accredited by the American National Standards Institute (ANSI) under the Conference for Food Protection Standards.
