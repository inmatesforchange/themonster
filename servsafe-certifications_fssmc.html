 SERVSAFE CERTIFICATIONS

Receive your Food Service Sanitation Manager Certification (FSSMC) with Spiral Group, Inc., and trust ServSafe—the only food safety program with roots in the foodservice industry to help you protect customer health, improve employee performance, and preserve your business reputation.

Preparing food for others is a big responsibility. The steps you follow, or neglect to follow, can adversely affect other people’s health and wellness. Consumers trust that the food they are served in your food and/or retail establishment is safe to eat. In this class, you will learn how to properly prepare and serve food so that you can maintain that trust.

In the ServSafe Food Protection Manager class, conducted by a Certified ServSafe Instructor, managers learn to implement essential food safety practices and create a culture of food safety. All content and materials are based on actual job tasks identified by foodservice industry experts. The information imparted to food service managers in this program is based on the FDA food code, food safety research, and years of food sanitation training experience. The program is designed and facilitated to provide sufficient food safety knowledge to protect the public from foodborne illness.

BENEFITS OF THE SERVSAFE® FOOD PROTECTION MANAGER CERTIFICATION:
Validates your professional credibility.
National accreditation as a Food Protection Manager offers you acceptance in all fifty states for regulatory requirements up to 16 hours.
Authenticates that you understand the tasks that employees, managers, and food safety professionals need to know to keep food safe in their establishment.
Your food safety knowledge can be shared with your entire operation.

Classes are available in English, Spanish, and Chinese and the exam is taken online for immediate results and same day certification. Computers or tablets must be provided.

Typically, the ServSafe® Food Protection Manager Classes are available for groups of 3 or more. One-to-One classes are also available for individuals. Give us a call if you require this option.

WHAT IS A FOOD PROTECTION MANAGER?
A food handler, or food employee, is an individual working with unpackaged food, food equipment or utensils, or food-contact surfaces. As of July 1, 2014, Section 750.570 of the Illinois Food Service Sanitation Code requires food handler training in certain conditions.

HOW THE SERVSAFE® FOOD HANDLER CERTIFICATE DIFFERS FROM THE FOOD PROTECTION MANAGER CERTIFICATION

The ServSafe® Food Handler Certificate verifies basic food safety knowledge and is for individuals in food handler employee-level positions. Upon successful completion of the ANSI ASTM 2659 accredited Food Handler course and 40-question assessment, the employee will receive a Certificate of Achievement from the National Restaurant Association that can be printed from a secure section of the ServSafe website.

The ServSafe® Food Protection Manager Certification verifies that a manager, or person-in-charge, has sufficient food safety knowledge to protect the public from foodborne illness. Individuals that successfully pass the 90-question, multiple-choice exam will receive a ServSafe® Food Protection Manager Certification. The ServSafe® Food Protection Manager Certification is accredited by the American National Standards Institute (ANSI) under the Conference for Food Protection Standards.
