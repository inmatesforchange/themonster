EMPLOYMENT DISCRIMINATION

COURSE OVERVIEW
Discriminatory Employment Practices include biased behavior in recruiting, hiring, job assignment, compensation, promotion, testing, training, apprenticeships, internships, and termination processes. Employment discrimination occurs silently in the beliefs, attitudes, and values that one applies in his mind toward a job candidate. Although discriminatory words may never be uttered, the person prone to discriminate will find a non-discriminatory way to reject a job seeker.

AT THE END OF THIS COURSE, THE STUDENT SHOULD BE ABLE TO:
Define discrimination and disability, as outlined by the law and the ADA.
Identify when a job opportunity has been denied to a person based on his relationship to, or association with, people included in the protected classifications.
Describe the types of discrimination disabled people are protected against.

WHO SHOULD ENROLL
Employers, Employment Recruiters, Hiring Decision-Makers, Workforce Managers, Social Workers, Case Managers, Persons Responsible for Conducting Interviews on the Behalf on an Employer, Staffing Agency Personnel.

WORKPLACE HARASSMENT

COURSE OVERVIEW
Workplace harassment violates our Civil Rights. The EEOC defines harassment as unwelcome verbal, physical, and sexual behavior. Harassing conduct includes offensive jokes, slurs, name calling, physical assaults or threats, intimidation, ridicule, insults, offensive pictures, and more. The harasser can be a supervisor, co-worker, customer, vendor, etc. The victim doesn't even have to be the person being harassed—it can be anyone adversely affected by the harassing behavior.

In this course, students learn how to recognize and address harassment. They begin analyzing their own behavior to make sure it's appropriate at all times. We also teach prevention methods that keep the workplace free from harassment.

AT THE END OF THIS COURSE, THE STUDENT SHOULD BE ABLE TO:
Define harassment in all its forms.
Understand the devastating effects harassment can have on victims, witnesses, and businesses.
Recognize and address harassment.
Know common prevention strategies against harassment.
Analyze his own behavior to ensure it is appropriate at all times.

WHO SHOULD ENROLL
All Professionals

WORKPLACE DIVERSITY & INCLUSION

COURSE OVERVIEW
If your assumptions about certain groups of people are based on what you've heard from others, then it is possible that those assumptions are incorrect. Sometimes, the beliefs we hold are the result of cultural conditioning. In this course, students are taught to accept that we all make mistakes. We can all work to become more aware of how our inner thoughts and feelings affect our beliefs, actions, and inactions.

AT THE END OF THIS COURSE, THE STUDENT SHOULD BE ABLE TO:
Define Worplace Diversity.
Understand the devastating effects stereotypes can have on us all.
Spend time observing the attitudes and behaviors that contribute to the exclusion of others.
Work to prevent behaviors that can be perceived as offensive.
