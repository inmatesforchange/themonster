THE MOST COMMON HEALTH CODE VIOLATIONS
Not Adhering to Proper Handwashing Procedures. Employees either washing their hands in a sink designated for food preparation, or not washing their hands at all when safe food handling has been interrupted.

Failure to properly label bottles containing chemicals.

Missing Food Service Sanitation Manager Certificate. The Health Code requires every food service establishment to employ a supervisor of food operations who has a non-expired Health Department FSSM Certificate.

Holding food at unsafe temperatures. The Health Code requires food service establishments to keep potentially hazardous food at safe temperatures. Although there are some exceptions, there are two options. Either 1. food can be kept below 41 or above 140 degrees Fahrenheit; or 2. food can be kept at room temperature for "brief periods" if the establishment monitors the time and temperature using time as a Public Health Control.

Food not protected from chemical, physical, and biological contaminants. The Health Code requires food service establishments to protect food from contamination during food storage, preparation, transportation, and display. Surfaces must be smooth and free from paint, rust, stains, or anything that can contaminate food and make it difficult to see whether the surface has been thoroughly cleaned.

Food Contact Surfaces NOT Prpoperly Cleaned & Sanitized. The Health Code requires food service establishments to keep food contact surfaces clean and sanitized to prevent contamination. Surfaces such as, cutting boards, utensils, deli meat slicers, and mixing bowls must be cleaned and sanitized after each use or following any activity where contamination may have occurred to prevent contamination and cross-contamination. In addition, equipment must be made of materials that are easily cleaned [and sanitized if required] and movable so that the surfaces, equipment, and the areas around them can be cleaned routinely.

Lack of Overall Cleanliness Due to Improper Maintenance. The Health Code requires food service establishments to keep those surfaces and areas that do not come in contact with food. To ensure overall cleanliness, the food service establishment must adhere to a proper cleaning and maintenance schedule. Adhering to an effective cleaning and maintenance program will likely prevent conditions that can lead to the entry and nesting of pests.
