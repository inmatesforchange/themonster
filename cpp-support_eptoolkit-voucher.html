GAPS IN EMPLOYMENT HISTORY FOR THE FORMERLY INCARCERATED - DEBUNKING THE MYTH
The Illinois state prison system requires inmates to acquire and maintain employment work during their entire period of incarceration. It is not unusual for inmates to contribute to the overall profitability of the prison system, as well as assist with achieving and maintaining Operational Excellence. Therefore, there aren't any employment gaps from the time the person is incarcerated until he is released from the prison system. In fact, it is very likely that many them learned new skills that are easily transferable to your organization.

It is unlikely that people with criminal records, who are also qualified to execute the duties of the positions you're seeking to fill, will return to a life of crime if allowed to earn a living with an Employer of Choice.
