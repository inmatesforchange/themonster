THE RETURNING CITIZEN.

Michelle Jones, our Founder, began the process of seeking meaningful and gainful employment as soon as her release from the Logan Correctional facility in August 2014. However, because of the parole restrictions and other prohibitive stipulations set forth by the parole board, her quest for immediate employment was short-lived. The numerous ineffective and time consuming activities required by the parole board caused her to become despondent and she thought about re-offending on a few occasions.

The Hardin House
In addition, she was placed in a recovery home due to homelessness. This is a standard practice for people who have been released from jail or prison and have no other housing options -whether or not recovery is required. The first recovery home, Hardin House located on Chicago's southside, was consumed with bedbugs, brown and white cockroaches, lice, mice, rats, and live mold. The ceiling in the bathroom on the first level had caved in while she was a resident there. Causing the contents of the flushed toilet on the second level to drain into the first floor's bathroom.

Daily meetings - NA, AA, CA, and PA - were required for everyone to attend. Whether they abused narcotics, alcohol, or cocaine. Even if you had no prior experience as a prostitute, residents had to attend these mandatory meetings and listen to the ridiculously candid testimonies of anyone willing to "anonymously" share their secrets about heinous acts and sycophancy that should be taken to their graves. In addition, the owner of Hardin House, Kathy Hardin, forced all the residents to illegally turn over their link cards (SNAP Benefits) as additional payment for living at the government funded recovery program. Needless to say, Michelle had been restricted to a recovery environment that was not at all conducive to recovery. (Kathy Hardin was unable to be found for several hours when Michelle went to retrieve her link card when she exited the program. The day Michelle left, there was over $100 remaining on her link card, when Kathy Hardin finally gave it back, she had depleted the card of its funds.)

The Gateway Kedzie House
As a result, on November 2, 2014, just three months after her release, Michelle and her parole agent decided that she be placed in a different recovery environment. One that would allow her to flourish and experience some semblance of recovery from the stalking and electronic harassment she incessantly endured by Joe Soto of Chicago's V103 and Clear Channel, which lead to her 2.5 year incarceration.

The place was the Gateway Kedzie House, located on Chicago's northwest side. After exposure to recovery-related activities at the second recovery home, Michelle began rebuilding her life from within and imagining how she could utilize her skills and previous work and life experiences to make a necessary contribution to society. She decided to return to the hospitality industry as either a manager, operations consultant, or trainer. This meant she had to regain the industry’s required certifications. So, she set out to do just that.

While a resident at the Gateway Kedzie House, Michelle was encouraged by her peers to seek the support and resources that Re-Entry programs provide. Most of these programs are funded by the government, and are supposed to assist the returning citizen (ex-offender) with successful re-entry back into society. These programs are, according to their mission and vision statements, structured to reduce an ex-offender's inclination toward recidivism by helping them find meaningful and gainful employment and by procuring housing.

The Safer Foundation
The first program Michelle attended was a 5-day job readiness training program at the Safer Foundation. Unfortunately, neither did the Safer Foundation assist Michelle with obtaining meaningful employment, nor did they help procure any housing. (Later, Michelle discovered that, according to the Chicago Housing Authority, people who are making attempts to re-enter society after incarceration cannot and will NEVER be able to be housed with their assistance — unless they have been incarcerated for prostitution, drugs, or gun possession.)

Michelle soon realized that her previous work experience and skill set had made one of the African-American job readiness instructors employed by the Safer Foundation uncomfortable. Most of Michelle's previous work experience was challenged, negated, and belittled by the instructor. She was adamant about NOT including Michelle's work experience and skill set on the resume she lackadaisically compiled for Michelle. In fact, during one of the classes, the instructor realized that Michelle had more experience than she and made a comment that Michelle could likely execute the duties of her own position if given the opportunity. Not surprisingly, that opportunity — and none like it with the Safer Foundation — was never provided. Nevertheless, since Michelle had lost everything as a result of her incarceration, she used that resume and began to build on it until it reflected her entire skill set and work history.

Michelle, facing homelessness again, was told about a better living environment by an intern instructor she had befriended while enrolled in the three-week job readiness training program at the Michael Barlow Center. The Greater Chicago Food Depository
In April 2015, Michelle began her job seeking quest, again. But to no avail. She believed the gaps in her work history, reflected on her resume, disqualified her from the managerial job opportunities for which she applied. As a result, Michelle decided to apply at the Greater Chicago Food Depository's Culinary program so that she could regain her FSSMC. However, Michelle was dismayed again. The African-American female responsible for testing and assisting ex-offenders with entry into this program had conducted an interview with Michelle after she passed the initial exam. She was upset that Michelle had passed th exam with flying colors. During the interview, she had one of her colleagues join them.

When the woman asked Michelle about why she had been incarcerated, Michelle shared the ordeal as best she could. When it was revealed that the so-called victim was Joe Soto of Chicago's V103, she became visibly upset that I would accuse him of stalking me. She asked me why would someone famous choose to stalk someone like me. I told her that she would have to ask him because there is NO way that I would be able to answer any questions regarding the mentality and intentions of another. As a result, she told me that I should submit any documents I may have received from psychiatrists and other mental health providers if The Greater Chicago Food Depository's Culinary Arts program would even consider her as a candidate. The program director told Michelle that her criminal past was a major obstacle. The program that was created to assist ex-offenders. Michelle did not go back to the facility after that conversation.

EDDR Corporation
In June 2015, Michelle enrolled in a different job readiness training program, one that seemed better suited to her career goals — EDDR Corporation — while a resident at HSI's Tabitha House. However, after a few days of attendance, Michelle realized she was becoming disillusioned by these government and corporate funded job readiness training programs. The items highlighted in the program’s flyer, those that piqued her interest, were well out of reach. A pattern of luring ex-offenders into their programs to deliver non-existent services was emerging. Each of the Job Readiness Training programs and Re-Entry Services Providers, whose core services were that of assisting returning citizens with re-entry services, housing, and gainful employment were just a cover for these companies to extract monies and other resources from wealthy persons, the government, and major corporations.

Oh well. Michelle decided to simply complete the EDDR Culinary training program to pass time, because none of these organizations were really going to help anyone but themselves. Besides, we never had any culinary training. After approximately two weeks into the program, the instructor resigned due to lack of pay (the government monies on which the program depended to operate were cut). Michelle saw this as a Divine Opportunity, and convinced the program’s executive director to allow her to fill in for the instructor — for free. After three weeks without pay, Michelle began receiving $100 per week as an intern instructor for approximately five weeks. She assisted many of the program’s participants with acquiring their food handler’s certifications by developing a training course and program curriculum. According to their website, EDDR claims to be proficient in the areas of Instructional Design, Change Management, Course Evaluation, Curriculum Development, Project Management, Training Development & Training, Technical Writing, and Research though Goldie (their CEO) never provided any of the items for which they claim to be their areas of specialty to Michelle when she was her intern instructor.

Several weeks later, Michelle was told that she could bypass Chef Toni and enroll in the FSSMC program, instead, in an effort to receive the Food Service Sanitation Manager Certification. SPIRAL GROUP'S INCEPTION

While enrolled as a student in their culinary arts program, Michelle was learning a lot about how EDDR operated and began dreaming of having her own NFP one day. After a mutual decision to depart from the organization that gave her the chance to be an intern instructor, she began creating her own job readiness training program in late September 2015. And because she had been enrolled in three well-known job readiness training programs, she knew that she would not make the same mistakes she encountered by administrators and instructors of these programs when building Spiral Group.

Her goal was to develop training modules pertinent to employment preparation and shorten the length of job readiness training, considerably. After many months of painstaking research; the development and subsequent revision of training modules; and the creation of a comprehensive website, in April 2016, Spiral Group, Inc. and SpiralGroup.org were born.
